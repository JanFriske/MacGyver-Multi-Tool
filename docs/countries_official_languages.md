### Notes
- **de facto**: Languages marked as "de facto" are widely used but not legally designated as official

## Regional Language Groups

### Africa
- **French**: 21 countries (former French colonies)
- **English**: 20+ countries (former British colonies)
- **Arabic**: North Africa (Algeria, Egypt, Libya, Morocco, Tunisia, etc.)
- **Portuguese**: Angola, Mozambique, Cape Verde, Guinea-Bissau, São Tomé

### Americas
- **Spanish**: Most of Latin America (18 countries)
- **English**: USA, Canada (with French), Caribbean nations
- **Portuguese**: Brazil
- **French**: Canada (with English), Haiti

### Asia
- **Arabic**: Middle East (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Yemen, Jordan, etc.)
- **Chinese**: China, Singapore, Taiwan
- **English**: India, Pakistan, Philippines, Singapore

### Europe
- **Germanic**: German, English, Dutch, Swedish, Norwegian, Danish, Icelandic
- **Romance**: French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, Romanian
- **Slavic**: Russian, Polish, Czech, Slovak, Bulgarian, Serbian, Croatian, Ukrainian

### Oceania
- **English**: Australia, New Zealand, Papua New Guinea, most Pacific islands
- **French**: Vanuatu, New Caledonia (territory)

## Sign Languages as Official Languages

Several countries recognize sign languages as official:
- **Iceland**: Icelandic Sign Language
- **New Zealand**: New Zealand Sign Language
- **Papua New Guinea**: PNG Sign Language
- **Philippines**: Filipino Sign Language
- **South Korea**: Korean Sign Language
- **Zimbabwe**: Zimbabwean Sign Languages
